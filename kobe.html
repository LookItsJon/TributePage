Kobe Bryant

The Black Mamba

Kobe, also known as the Black Mamba, modeling his sweet threads.

Here's a list of Kobe's accomplishments:

  • 1996 - Graduated from Lower Merion High School and was drafted directly into the NBA by the Charlotte Hornets as the 13th overall draft pick. The drafting rights were then traded to the Lakers.
  • 1997 - Won the 1997 Slam Dunk Contest during the All-Star weekend, becoming the youngest dunk champion ever at the age of 18.
  • 1998 - During the 1998-99 season, Bryant started every game, making his mark as the premier guard of the league.
  • 1999-2002 - Kobe and Shaquille O'Neal led the Lakers to win three consecutive championships.
  • 2004 - The Lakers make it to the NBA Finals, but lose in an upset to the Detroit Pistons in Game 5. Coach Phil Jackson leaves the Lakers and co-star O'Neal is traded to the Heat. Kobe re-signed with the Lakers after turning down an offer from the Clippers.
  • 2005 - Phil Jackson returned to the Lakers and worked with Kobe to improve the team.
  • 2006 - Scored Career high of 81 points in one game. Also in 2006, Kobe changed his number from 8 to 24.
  • 2007 - Reached 20,000 total career points scored.
  • 2009 - Kobe led the Lakers to win the NBA finals against the Orlando Magic.
  • 2010 - Kobe and the Lakers defeat the Boston Celtics in the NBA Finals.
  • 2012 - Dwayne Wade broke his nose in the 2012 All-Star Game.
  • 2013-2015 - Plagued with injuries that prevented him from playing for most of the seasons.
  • 2016 - Retired, with his last game played against the Utah Jazz, in which he led the Lakers to victory with 60 points.
